What is Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu?

Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu (BJJ) is a martial art originally developed by the Gracie family, that allows a proficient fighter to defeat a bigger, stronger, opponent through the use of efficiency and leverage. Although defence from strikes are practised, the sport is a grappling art. This means we don't punch or kick each other, and the risk of injuries that comes with other Martial Arts that do are greatly diminished. Through the use of techniques you are taught in class, you will learn how to take your opponent to the ground, control them, and submit them. The hardest part of BJJ is having the courage to put yourself out of your comfort zone and show up to your first class. At Alton BJJ we are always happy to see new faces, and welcome you to our Academy.

Meet the team

Brian is the head instructor and founder of Alton BJJ. He holds a black belt under Andy Roberts, and has spent many years practising in and out of the Gi.

John Andrews

John teaches a fundamentals class and hosts an Open mat in his secret ninja barn in Medstead. Training since 1984, John holds a brown belt under Andy Roberts, a Black Belt in Japanese Jiu-Jitsu, and is a veteran martial artist.

Matt Whyton

Matt holds a black belt under Andy Roberts, and has been training martial arts for many years. After retiring from his boxing career, Matt offers his service as a professional Boxing and MMA coach.

Andy Roberts

Andy has been training martial arts since he was 8 years old and started teaching professionally when he left school. He founded Andy Roberts BJJ in Sept 2007, and is a Black Belt under 10x world champion Roger Gracie. Alton BJJ is an affiliate of Andy Roberts BJJ.
